The Details

At LUNA, we aim to empower teenage girls and help break the stigma currently surrounding periods. We believe that a girl’s time of the month should never evoke feelings of embarrassment or uncertainty. Menstruation especially shouldn’t be the cause of girl’s missing school due to a lack of resources or shame in this natural occurrence. We plan to use our business to highlight and assist in the relief of issues such as gender equality, the lack of adequate resources for girls in developing countries, and the lack of women’s health education girls in these countries receive.

Percentages

52% of girls reported feeling self-conscious when they on their period

75% of girls do not change their cloth at all during a school day (unhygienic)

95% of women in Bangladesh cannot afford sanitary napkins

How Can We Help

In Bangladesh, we will partner with Ella Pad which produces low-cost sanitary napkins for girls and women made of the tons of garment waste that collects in Bangladesh. Ella Pad empowers women by not only providing jobs for those working to produce the pads but also an easily accessible option for girls working in factories who previously would have had to take off from work due to a lack of period products. This helps promote the local economy as well as create a supply of pads in areas where menstrual products would otherwise be inaccessible.
